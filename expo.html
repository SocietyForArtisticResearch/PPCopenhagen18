The Research Catalogue Exposition as a Digital Object: Challenges and Future

Luc Döbereiner

December 13, 2018

RC Exposition as a Digital Object

  • Untapped potential
  • Publication format and an authoring framework
  • Links human authoring and consumption with machine processing
  • Can be processes, programmed, transformed and linked and extended
  • Different milieux: aesthetic, political, technological and social context
  • Exposition editor as a research tool

Challenge 1: Sketching and Publishing

  • Stability, persistence and referencability have been prioritised
  • Hampers drafting and sketching
  • A text cannot exist without a width, height and position
  • Productiveness of the indeterminate in experimental practices
  • Need to accommodate the tentative and fragmentary

Challenge 2: Absolute Positioning

  • Fixedness of the exposition format stands in contrast to HTML and web technologies
  • Inherited the ideal of static appearance from print publishing
  • Technologies and ways of reading are constantly evolving
  • Rethink the role of the author, dynamic potential should be embraced

Challenge 3: Dominance of the Visual

  • Design of an exposition is almost exclusively visual
  • Proximity, size, pathways, topography are vital to the meaning of an exposition
  • We should seek sonorous, textual, temporal etc. ways of structuring expositions
  • Exposition needs to be a programmable object that can be linked, rendered, stored and converted in infinite ways

Web Pages

  • A WP is not an autonomous two-dimensional canvas, but a dynamically changing data structure
  • A WP depends on a multitude of standards and services
  • A WP interacts with servers, clients and events
  • A WP needs to be performed by browsers

Multiplicity

  • Need for a formal format specification
  • Multiple authoring tools
  • Greatly enhances the long-term accessibility and digital preservation
  • Content not tied to a single implementation

Adaptability

The control which designers know in the print medium, and often desire in the web medium, is simply a function of the limitation of the printed page. We should embrace the fact that the web doesn’t have the same constraints, and design for this flexibility.
(John Allsopp, “A Dao of Web Design”, 2000)

Adaptability

  • Authoring as exploration of potentials
  • Rethink the exposition as a more dynamic entity
  • Access for people with vision impairment or hearing loss

Programmability

  • Web pages are programmable entitites
  • Open expositions to creative coders
  • Departure from WYSIWYG
  • Investigation of interface design and usability
  • Exposition as a dynamic digital object that can connect to other objects, services, technologies and humans
